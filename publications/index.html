  1. Corros. Sci.
    Study on the corrosion behavior of copper coupled with TiO2 with different crystal structures
    Xinyu Zhang, Sijia Li, Wen Sun, Lida Wang, Jing Wang, and Guichang Liu
    Corrosion Science, 2021
    Abstract Bib HTML PDF

    The influences of semi-conductive TiO2 with different crystal structures on the initial corrosion behavior of copper in dark conditions were investigated. Results showed that anatase TiO2 showed “corrosion-promotion activity” to copper, while rutile TiO2 did not. It is revealed that the “corrosion-promotion activity” is associated with TiO2 crystal structure and mainly dominated by the work function of TiO2. When the work function of n-type TiO2 is higher than that of copper, electrons are inclined to transfer from copper to TiO2. In addition, higher electrical conductivity and oxygen reduction reaction activity of TiO2 facilitate the electron consumption at the TiO2/electrolyte interface.

  2. Mater. Prot.
    Effect of Corrosion Products of Pure Iron on the Corrosion Behavior of Pure Iron and Its Mechanism
    Xinyu Zhang, Lida Wang, Wen Sun, Yongpeng He, Xiangquan Shu, and Guichang Liu
    Materials Protection (Chinese Letters), 2021
    Abstract Bib HTML PDF

    The effects of Fe3O4, β-FeOOH and γ-FeOOH on the corrosion behavior of pure iron were investigated using scanning vibration electrode technique (SVET). Moreover, based on the semiconducting properties of the corrosion products, the mechanism of this effect was studied by scanning Kelvin probe (SKP), Mott-Schottky curve, and oxygen reduction polarization curve. Results showed that all three corrosion products could promote pure iron corrosion, and the promotion effect of three corrosion products on pure iron corrosion weakened in order of β-FeOOH, Fe3O4, and γ-FeOOH. The corrosion-promotion effect of corrosion products on pure iron depends on two factors: the relative magnitude of the work function of corrosion product and the work function of pure iron ，and the oxygen reduction activity of the corrosion product. When the work function of corrosion product is higher than that of pure iron, electrons are inclined to transfer from metal to corrosion product, accelerating the anodic reaction of corrosion cell. In addition, the higher the oxygen reduction reaction activity of the corrosion product, the more favorable to the electron consumption, promoting the cathodic reaction of corrosion cell.
